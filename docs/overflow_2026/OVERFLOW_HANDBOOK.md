## Award Structure

Prizes for Sui Overflow 2026 will follow a **split distribution model** designed to encourage continued development beyond the hackathon:

- **50% of the prize** will be awarded upon announcement of winners
- **50% of the prize** will be awarded after successful mainnet deployment

If a winning team has already deployed their project to mainnet by the time winners are announced in August, they will receive **100% of the prize upfront**.

## Notes

- Mainnet deployment must meet the minimum functional requirements as defined by the Sui team and/or track sponsors.
- Teams are encouraged to continue building post-hackathon to unlock the full prize amount.
- Additional support, opportunities, and ecosystem pathways may be available for top-performing teams.

## FAQ

- **What are the eligibility requirements to participate in Overflow?**
    - The project must be deployed to Sui mainnet or testnet by the time of shortlisting and demo day.
    - The submission must either:
        - be a completely new project built between **May 7th to June 21st, 2026**, or
        - introduce substantial new functionality, features, or integrations to an existing Sui project developed during the same period.
    - The project team must be available to participate in the virtual Demo Day session.
    - At least one member of the project team must pass KYC in order to receive prizes.
    - Participants residing in OFAC-sanctioned regions are not eligible to participate and win.
- **Is Demo Day in-person?**
    
    No, Demo Day for Sui Overflow 2026 will be conducted virtually. Shortlisted teams will present their projects remotely to judges and ecosystem partners.
    
- **What if my project fits into multiple tracks?**
    - Projects may naturally overlap across multiple tracks. However, each submission may only be submitted under one primary track. Teams should choose the track that best represents the core focus and value proposition of their project.
- **Within the tracks, there are subtracks. Are subtracks competing separately?**
    
    No, subtracks are intended only as guides or inspiration to help teams decide what type of project to build within a broader track theme. Projects will still compete within the main track itself, and winners will be selected across the overall track regardless of subtrack category.
    
- **Can I be a part of multiple teams?**
    
    Yes, participants may contribute to multiple projects or teams, provided the projects are meaningfully different and are not simply replicas or slight variations of each other.
    
- **Can I reuse part of my code from my previous work?**
    
    Yes, if you have the legal rights to the code, you may reuse components such as frontend frameworks, UI libraries, boilerplate infrastructure, or tooling. However, projects should still demonstrate substantial new development completed during the hackathon period. If significant portions of the project were pre-existing, teams should clearly disclose this in their submission.
    
- **Do I need to open-source my project repo?**
    
    Open-sourcing your repository is not required. However, teams may be asked to provide temporary repository access to judges during the review and evaluation process.
    
- **How are projects evaluated?**
    
    Projects will primarily be evaluated based on:
    
    - Product & UX
    - Real-world application
    - Technical implementation
    - Presentation & vision
    
    Judges may also consider meaningful usage of Sui-specific capabilities and long-term ecosystem potential depending on the track.
    
- **Are AI-assisted tools allowed?**
    
    Yes, AI-assisted development tools are allowed and expected. Projects will not be judged based on the amount of manually written code, but rather on product quality, technical execution, creativity, usability, and real-world value.
